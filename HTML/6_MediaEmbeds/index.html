Tutorial Video: Spanish Vowel Sounds

Spanish has five vowel sounds that are short, crisp and never change their sound like English vowels do. Watch this instructional video to learn the proper pronunciation of each Spanish vowel.

Remember to practice each vowel sound multiple times. The key difference between Spanish and English vowels is that Spanish vowels maintain the same sound regardless of their position in a word.

Audio Practice: The Spanish 'R'

The Spanish 'R' sound is one of the most challenging sounds for English speakers to master. Listen to these audio examples and practice your pronunciation.

Single 'R' sound - as in "pero" (but)
pero (but), caro (expensive), hora (hour)
Pronunciation Tip:

The single 'R' in Spanish is similar to the 'tt' sound in the American English pronunciation of "butter" or "better" - a quick tap of the tongue against the roof of your mouth.

Rolled 'RR' sound - as in "perro" (dog)
perro (dog), carro (car), correr (to run)
Pronunciation Tip:

For the rolled 'RR', position your tongue behind your top front teeth and blow air to make your tongue vibrate. Practice saying "tt-tt-tt" quickly to get the feel for the tongue position.
